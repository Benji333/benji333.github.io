Construction

1832 - 1862

Construction of the Manor: The construction of the Adare Manor began in 1832 by the 2nd Earl of Dunraven, Windham Henry Quin. The manor was designed by the renowned architect James Pain and was completed in 1862.

Restoration

1987

Restoration by Tom Kane: Adare Manor fell into disrepair and was eventually abandoned until it was purchased in 1987 by Irish businessman Tom Kane. Kane invested over €30 million in restoring the manor to its former glory, including converting it into a luxury hotel.

Golf Course

2000

Golf Course Construction: In 2000, Adare Manor unveiled its new golf course designed by the legendary Robert Trent Jones Sr. The course quickly became one of the top courses in Ireland and hosted the Irish Open in 2007 and 2008.

Renovation

2017

Renovation by ReardonSmith Architects: Adare Manor underwent a massive renovation in 2017, which took 21 months to complete. The renovation was carried out by ReardonSmith Architects and cost over €70 million. The manor was closed during this time and reopened in November 2017 with new rooms, a ballroom, and a spa.

Ryder Cup

2018

Hosted the Ryder Cup Bid: In 2018, Adare Manor submitted a bid to host the 2026 Ryder Cup. The bid was successful, and Adare Manor will be the venue for the 2026 Ryder Cup, which will be the first time the tournament will be held in Ireland. The announcement of the successful bid has put Adare Manor on the global stage as one of the top golf destinations in the world.
